# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

APDL contributors, reviewers, and maintainers commit to a respectful,
welcoming, harassment-free project. Examples of constructive participation
include:

- giving specific, evidence-based technical feedback;
- respecting different backgrounds, identities, experience levels, and points
  of view;
- accepting correction, acknowledging mistakes, and focusing on the work; and
- protecting private, personal, security-sensitive, and embargoed information.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, threats, discriminatory or sexual
language or imagery, deliberate intimidation, sustained disruption, doxxing,
publishing private information without permission, or retaliation against a
good-faith reporter.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, release and
support channels, project events, and when someone publicly represents APDL or
uses project authority.

## Reporting and Enforcement

Raise ordinary concerns with a maintainer in the relevant issue or pull request.
If a public report would expose or endanger someone, contact maintainers
privately through the repository's
[private advisory form](https://github.com/kuvera-apdl/apdl/security/advisories/new)
and begin the title with `Conduct report`. Do not post sensitive evidence
publicly.

Maintainers will limit disclosure to people needed to review the report, give
all parties a fair opportunity to provide context when safe, and act according
to severity and pattern. Responses may include a private warning, content
removal, temporary participation limits, loss of project role, or a permanent
ban. A maintainer named in a report must recuse themselves from handling it.

Good-faith reports are protected from retaliation. Knowingly false or
malicious reports are themselves a violation of this policy.
